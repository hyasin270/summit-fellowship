## Phase 2: Narrative & Flow Refinements (IN PROGRESS)

### Context (from "The Middle Innings" essay + Ahson Rabbani transcript)

The deeper philosophy behind Summit Fellowship:
- **The collective, not any one org.** The mission is cultivating a "garden of organizations" all pushing toward the same impossible goals. Summit Fellowship is one act of gardening.
- **Events are touchpoints, not the strategy.** Oxford, Vancouver, Skardu are nodes in a constellation — ways to help the right people find each other in different rooms around the world.
- **Pakistan's narrative is the real product.** The single story about Pakistan needs to change. Every room, every introduction, every funder who visits Skardu chips away at that.
- **Never pitch.** The work speaks. The mountains speak. The founders speak. The website should feel like an invitation to something already happening.

### 2a. Behind Section — De-center Taleemabad, Lead with People

**Current:** Two org cards — Taleemabad (Organizer) and Mulago Foundation (Framework). Taleemabad-centric. Reads like a corporate "about us."

**New approach:** Lead with the people behind this, not the orgs. The people are the story. The orgs are backdrop.

Key people to feature (without making it a bio page):
- **Dr. Ahson Rabbani** — ChildLife Foundation. Recent Skoll Awardee. Runs 13 pediatric ERs treating 1.7M children annually. His organization's Skoll Award is the anchor for the Oxford gathering.
- **Haroon Yasin** — Skoll Foundation staff. Built Taleemabad to reach millions of Pakistani children. The fellowship that shaped his thinking is now being brought to Pakistan.
- **Nadir Shams** — Ex-Skoll Foundation Managing Director. Career spanning One Acre Fund, Spring Impact, Dalberg. Managing the Oxford invite list and funder outreach.
- **Kevin Starr** — Mulago Foundation founder. His Scale Screen framework is the intellectual backbone. More optimistic about Pakistan than anyone. Climbed Trango Towers in '95.
- **Sarah Farooq** — Former COO of Taleemabad, now running Acumen's global fellowship. Designing the Skardu program.

**Framing:** These are people who have spent their careers at the intersection of scale, impact, and Pakistan. They found each other. This is what they're building together. Not "Taleemabad presents" — more like "a group of people who kept showing up in the same rooms decided to build one of their own."

**Keep Mulago Foundation** as a separate card — they're the framework/curriculum partner and that's a meaningful distinction. But reframe Taleemabad card → something more collective.

### 2b. 3-Event Flow — The Site Tells the Story of All Three

**Current problem:** The site is structured as a Skardu fellowship website with a constellation section tacked on at the end. The 7-day program, Skardu section, and most content assumes one event.

**Fix:** The constellation should be the organizing principle. Each event is a chapter.

**Specific changes:**

1. **Move Constellation section up** — from after Funders to right after the Challenge/Opportunity section. It sets up the "what are we actually doing" before diving into details.

2. **Challenge section** — Keep but subtly reframe. Not just "Pakistan needs scale" but "Pakistan has extraordinary founders who are mostly invisible to global capital. We're changing that — through a deliberate series of gatherings."

3. **About section (7-day intensive)** — This is specifically Skardu. That's fine, but add a brief intro line that positions it as "the flagship event in the constellation."

4. **Program section** — Skardu-specific. Keep as-is.

5. **Skardu section** — Keep as-is. The setting sells itself.

6. **Behind section** — Rewrite per 2a above.

7. **CTA section** — Currently very Skardu-focused ("Express Interest"). Could include a lighter touch for Oxford/Vancouver: "For Oxford and Vancouver, reach out directly."

### 2c. Section Order (Proposed)
